career goal


Pursuing Excellence in Artificial Intelligence Engineering


My aspiration to become an Artificial Intelligence Engineer stems from a profound fascination with neural architecture search (NAS) and attention mechanisms in transformer-based models. The mathematical elegance of gradient descent optimization using adaptive moment estimation (Adam) and the challenge of preventing vanishing gradients through LSTM/GRU architectures drive my intellectual curiosity.


Through implementing convolutional neural networks (CNNs) with batch normalization and dropout regularization, I've gained practical experience in hyperparameter tuning using Bayesian optimization. My recent exploration of graph neural networks (GNNs) for non-Euclidean data representation and contrastive learning techniques in self-supervised learning scenarios has solidified my commitment to deep learning research.


I aim to specialize in developing multi-modal AI systems that integrate vision transformers (ViTs) with bidirectional encoder representations (BERT). Particular interests include optimizing model parallelism for distributed training and implementing quantization-aware training for edge deployment. My long-term vision involves contributing to neuromorphic computing architectures that bridge the gap between artificial and biological neural networks.


Technical Focus Areas:

  • ▸ Transformer-based architectures (BERT, GPT, T5)
  • ▸ Differentiable neural architecture search (DNAS)
  • ▸ Federated learning with differential privacy
